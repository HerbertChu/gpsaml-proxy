SAML-aware GlobalProtect proxy

Browser does the SAML.
Bastion does the tunnel.

Authenticate with corp SSO once in your browser. The extension captures the GlobalProtect cookie and hands it to this bastion, which spins up an isolated openconnect tunnel and gives you back a one-shot .command file. Double-click and your laptop reaches corp-internal services at their normal URLs — no VPN client running on your laptop.

01Install the extension

extension.zip Chrome MV3 unpacked extension
Download
  1. Unzip somewhere stable — Chrome needs the folder to stay on disk.
  2. Open chrome://extensions, toggle Developer mode on (top right).
  3. Click Load unpacked and pick the unzipped folder.
  4. Pin the extension to the toolbar.

02Connect

  1. Click the extension icon. Type your portal hostname, hit Authenticate via SAML.
  2. Sign in to corp SSO in the popped-up window — it closes itself when done.
  3. Pick a gateway from the dropdown (saved for next time).
  4. Chrome downloads a .command file. Right-click → Open the first time (Gatekeeper). Type your sudo password.
  5. Terminal stays foreground. Browse the registered hosts at their normal URLs. Ctrl+C to disconnect.

04Manual mode (no sudo)

If your laptop user can't sudo for /etc/hosts or port 443, the extension popup also surfaces a copy-pasteable ssh -L command using high local ports — access services at https://git.example.com:8443/. Cert validates because the hostname matches.

Security note. The bastion mints a single-use SSH key per session, and the .command writes / restores /etc/hosts in a trapped lifecycle. Closing the Terminal window cleans up everything.
